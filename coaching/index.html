The Framework

What Is IFS?

Internal Family Systems is an evidence-based model that understands the mind as naturally multiple. We all have different "parts": the inner critic, the people-pleaser, the part that shuts down under stress, the one that takes over in conflict.

IFS doesn't try to eliminate these parts. Instead, it helps you build a relationship with them. When you understand why a part does what it does, it no longer needs to run the show. What emerges is what IFS calls "Self," a state of clarity, calm, curiosity, and compassion.

In a professional context, this is the difference between reacting and leading.

1:1 Coaching

Individual Work for Leaders

Private sessions for leaders and professionals who are ready to go deeper. We work with the patterns that keep showing up: the avoidance, the overwork, the conflict loops, the quiet burnout. We trace them to their source.

  • Weekly or biweekly sessions, 60–75 minutes
  • Tailored to your specific challenges and goals
  • Integrates IFS with leadership development and somatic awareness
  • Confidential, non-judgmental, and surprisingly practical
  • Results that show up in how you lead, communicate, and make decisions

Group Coaching

Shared Work, Deeper Impact

Small-group coaching for teams or cohorts of leaders. The group setting adds a powerful dimension: you don't just learn about your own patterns. You see them reflected in others. This builds empathy, shared language, and the kind of trust that changes how people work together.

  • 4–8 participants per group
  • Facilitated by two experienced IFS practitioners
  • Combines teaching, live demonstration, and guided practice
  • Ideal for leadership cohorts, peer groups, or team subsets
